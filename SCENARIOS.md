# Hollywood Mogul - Starting Scenarios Guide

## Overview

The Scenario System adds 7 unique starting scenarios to Hollywood Mogul, each offering a completely different gameplay experience with distinct challenges, goals, and mechanics. Players can choose their scenario when starting a new game, dramatically increasing replayability.

## Available Scenarios

### 1. Classic Start (Normal Difficulty)
**The traditional Hollywood Mogul experience**

- **Starting Year:** 1933
- **Cash:** $410,000
- **Reputation:** 50/100
- **Special Rules:** None - balanced standard gameplay
- **Victory:** Survive until 1949 with positive cash and reputation

**Perfect for:** First-time players, classic tycoon experience

---

### 2. Poverty Row (Hard Difficulty)
**Build a studio empire from the bottom**

- **Starting Year:** 1935
- **Cash:** $150,000
- **Reputation:** 25/100
- **Starting Assets:** Western backlot (for cheap westerns)
- **Special Rules:**
  - Can only greenlight films under $75,000 until reputation reaches 40
  - Production time reduced by 20%
  - B-movie revenue bonus: +15%
  - Maximum budget increases with reputation
- **Victory:** Reach $500,000 cash, 50 reputation, and produce 15 films

**Perfect for:** Players who love underdog stories and resource management

**Strategy Tips:**
- Churn out cheap B-movies quickly to build cash
- Focus on westerns, horror, and serials
- Every successful film improves reputation
- Don't attempt A-pictures until you can afford them

---

### 3. The Inheritance (Hard Difficulty)
**Save your uncle's legacy from bankruptcy**

- **Starting Year:** 1938
- **Cash:** $800,000
- **Reputation:** 70/100
- **Debt:** $500,000 (must pay off)
- **Starting Assets:** 2 sound stages, western & NYC backlots
- **Special Rules:**
  - Debt payments of $50,000 due every 6 months
  - Bank forecloses if debt exceeds $700,000
  - Reputation loss if you drop below 50
  - Must maintain prestige film quality
- **Victory:** Pay off all debt while maintaining 60+ reputation

**Perfect for:** Players who enjoy balancing prestige with profit

**Strategy Tips:**
- Schedule debt payments around major film releases
- Make commercial hits between prestige films
- Never let reputation drop - it's your greatest asset
- Consider taking smaller profits to maintain quality

---

### 4. War Effort (Normal Difficulty)
**Navigate Hollywood during World War II**

- **Starting Year:** December 1942
- **Cash:** $350,000
- **Reputation:** 45/100
- **Starting Assets:** NYC backlot
- **Special Rules:**
  - Government contracts available for war films
  - Reduced male actor availability (draft)
  - Patriotic films receive +25% box office bonus
  - Material shortages: +10% production costs (1942-1945)
  - All films must support war effort (censorship)
  - Post-1945 transition bonus for peacetime films
- **Victory:** Survive to 1949 with $300k, 50 reputation, and 3 patriotic films

**Perfect for:** Players interested in historical scenarios

**Strategy Tips:**
- Government contracts provide steady income
- Use female leads and older actors during wartime
- Patriotic films are both profitable and required
- Plan for peacetime transition in 1945-46

---

### 5. The Blacklist (Very Hard Difficulty)
**Survive the Red Scare without losing your soul**

- **Starting Year:** October 1947
- **Cash:** $500,000
- **Reputation:** 60/100
- **Starting Assets:** 2 sound stages, western backlot
- **Special Rules:**
  - Random talent called before HUAC
  - Choice events: cooperate, resist, or stay neutral
  - Cooperation damages integrity but protects reputation
  - Resistance damages reputation but maintains integrity
  - Blacklisted talent cannot work
  - Public opinion affects box office
  - **Integrity Score** system (starts at 75)
- **Victory:** Survive to 1949 with $200k, 40 reputation, AND 50+ integrity

**Perfect for:** Players who want moral choices to matter

**Strategy Tips:**
- Every choice has consequences - there are no perfect answers
- Safe, apolitical films reduce HUAC scrutiny
- Defending talent may cost money but preserves integrity
- Your final score reflects your moral choices
- Multiple endings based on integrity vs. success balance

---

### 6. Studio Boss (Normal Difficulty)
**Manage a brilliant but difficult director**

- **Starting Year:** 1933
- **Cash:** $250,000
- **Reputation:** 40/100
- **Special Asset:** Orson Wexler (legendary director under exclusive contract)
- **Special Rules:**
  - Director demands: +30% budget, final cut, hand-picked cast
  - Director bonus: +20 quality to all their films
  - Must make at least 1 film per year with director (or they leave)
  - Director leaves if reputation drops below 35
  - Random events: tantrums, walk-offs, moments of brilliance
  - High Oscar potential on all director films
- **Victory:** Win 3 Oscars with your director, produce 8 films with them

**Perfect for:** Players who want a personality-driven narrative

**Strategy Tips:**
- Give the director what they need - they're worth it
- Budget for high costs but expect high returns
- Make commercial films between director projects to build cash
- Keep them happy or lose your golden goose
- Aim for Oscar season releases

---

### 7. Fresh Start (Very Hard Difficulty)
**The ultimate challenge - build from nothing**

- **Starting Year:** 1933
- **Cash:** $200,000
- **Reputation:** 30/100
- **Starting Assets:** 1 basic sound stage only
- **Special Rules:**
  - All costs increased by 15%
  - No starting contracts or premium scripts
  - Lower quality scripts available initially
  - Slower reputation gains
  - Actors demand higher salaries
  - Banks charge higher interest rates
  - **Achievement unlocked for completing this mode**
- **Victory:** Survive to 1949 with $100k+, 40+ reputation, and 20 films

**Perfect for:** Masochists and achievement hunters

**Strategy Tips:**
- Start with the cheapest films available
- Build cash reserves before taking any risks
- AVOID DEBT - interest rates are brutal
- Every decision matters - there's no margin for error
- This is Hollywood on hard mode - good luck!

## How to Use Scenarios

### Starting a New Game

1. Click "START NEW STUDIO" or restart from game over
2. The scenario selection screen appears automatically
3. Browse the available scenarios - click for more details
4. Select your desired scenario
5. Begin playing with unique starting conditions

### During Gameplay

- **Scenario Indicator:** A widget in the top-right corner shows your current scenario, difficulty, and objective
- **Click the indicator** to view detailed victory progress
- **Victory Tracking:** Check your progress toward scenario-specific goals at any time
- **Victory Notification:** Automatic alert when you achieve scenario victory
- **Continue Playing:** Even after scenario victory, you can continue until 1949

### Victory Conditions

Each scenario has unique victory conditions displayed in the selection screen and tracked during gameplay:

- **Cash targets:** Accumulate or maintain specific cash amounts
- **Reputation goals:** Reach and maintain reputation levels
- **Film quotas:** Produce a certain number or type of films
- **Debt elimination:** Pay off inherited debts
- **Specialty goals:** Oscar wins, integrity scores, etc.

### Special Mechanics by Scenario

#### Budget Restrictions (Poverty Row)
- Maximum film budget starts at $75,000
- Increases as reputation grows:
  - 40 reputation: $100,000 max
  - 50 reputation: $150,000 max
  - 60+ reputation: No restrictions

#### Debt Payment Schedule (The Inheritance)
- $50,000 payment due every 6 months (26 weeks)
- Payment automatically deducted from cash
- Warning alerts 4 weeks before payment
- Bank forecloses if total debt exceeds $700,000

#### Wartime Mechanics (War Effort)
- Government contracts appear in script library
- Male actors marked as "Draft Eligible"
- Patriotic themes required 1942-1945
- Peacetime transition events trigger in 1945-1946

#### Integrity System (The Blacklist)
- Integrity score: 0-100 (starts at 75)
- Choice events affect integrity and reputation
- Final ending determined by integrity + success combination
- Blacklisted talent become unavailable

#### Director Relationship (Studio Boss)
- Director satisfaction meter
- Monthly relationship events
- Tantrum risk if demands not met
- Bonus quality on all director films
- Contract renewal mechanics

#### Hard Mode Multipliers (Fresh Start)
- All production costs: +15%
- Actor salaries: +20%
- Loan interest rates: +25%
- Script quality: -10 average
- Reputation gains: -25%

## Technical Implementation

### Files Created

1. `/js/systems/scenarios.js` - Core scenario system
2. `/js/ui/scenario-ui.js` - UI components for scenario display
3. `/css/scenarios.css` - Styling for scenario selection and tracking

### Integration Points

- **game-state.js:** Modified to support scenario-based initialization
- **integration.js:** Added scenario UI initialization and victory checking
- **index.html:** Added scripts and stylesheets

### Game State Extensions

When a scenario is active, the game state includes:

```javascript
gameState.scenario = {
    id: 'scenario_id',
    name: 'Scenario Name',
    difficulty: 'normal|hard|very_hard',
    victoryConditions: { /* ... */ },
    specialRules: [ /* ... */ ],
    restrictions: [ /* ... */ ],
    bonuses: [ /* ... */ ],
    startDate: Date
}
```

### API Methods

**ScenarioSystem:**
- `getAllScenarios()` - Get all available scenarios
- `getScenarioById(id)` - Get specific scenario
- `applyScenario(id, gameState)` - Apply scenario to game state
- `checkVictoryConditions(gameState)` - Check if victory achieved
- `getVictoryProgress(gameState)` - Get progress toward victory
- `showScenarioSelection()` - Display selection UI
- `showScenarioDetails(id)` - Show detailed scenario info

**ScenarioUI:**
- `init()` - Initialize UI components
- `addScenarioIndicator()` - Add scenario widget to screen
- `showScenarioProgress()` - Display victory progress modal
- `checkVictoryConditions()` - Check and notify victory

## Balancing Notes

### Difficulty Calibration

**Normal:** Designed to be completable by average players with some Hollywood Mogul experience

**Hard:** Requires strategic planning and resource management. Expect 1-2 failed attempts before success.

**Very Hard:** Designed for experienced players. Expect multiple attempts. Success rate target: 20-30%.

### Playtesting Feedback

Each scenario has been balanced to provide:
- **15-30 minutes** of unique gameplay differences
- **Distinct strategic choices** not available in other scenarios
- **Meaningful decision points** that affect outcome
- **Clear progress feedback** toward victory
- **Multiple viable strategies** for completion
